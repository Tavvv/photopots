Features

Small app. Serious separation.

Everything here exists to keep work photos findable and personal photos personal.

  • Custom pots

    Name them, color them, make as many as you need. "Job — Miller St", "Rentals", "Family". Your pots, your call.

  • GPS metadata preserved

    Sorting never strips location data. The photo of the site stays pinned to the site — useful when a client asks "where was this taken?"

  • OCR search

    Search the words inside your photos. Type "invoice" or a serial number and get the exact receipt or nameplate, not 400 maybes.

  • One-tap share

    Send a job photo to a client, a receipt to your accountant, a class photo to a parent — from the pot, in one tap, anywhere.

  • Crop without losing metadata

    Crop out the messy background, straighten the whiteboard. Every byte of metadata — GPS included — stays right where it was.

  • iCloud sync

    Sort on your phone at the job site, find it on your Mac at the desk. Pots sync across iPhone, iPad, and Mac through your iCloud.

  • Batch screenshot triage

    Screenshots pile up fast. Review them in a batch: keep the useful ones in a pot, clear the rest. Done in minutes.

  • Widget & share extension

    A home-screen widget jumps you straight into a pot. The share extension sends photos from any app into one — no detours.

FAQ

Fair questions

Do sorted photos really leave my Camera Roll?

Yes. Sorting moves the original photo into the pot — it is removed from your Camera Roll, not just hidden in an album. Your Camera Roll stays personal, and your pots hold the rest.

Do photos taken with the in-app camera touch my Camera Roll?

No. When you capture straight into a pot, the photo is saved directly into that pot and never appears in your Camera Roll at all.

Will I lose GPS location or other metadata?

No. PhotoPots preserves your photo metadata, including GPS location, even when you crop or edit a photo inside a pot.

Can I share or move a photo out of a pot?

Yes. Any photo in a pot can be shared anywhere with one tap — messages, email, or back to your Camera Roll if you want it there.

How does PhotoPots sync across my devices?

PhotoPots syncs your pots through your iCloud account, so the same pots and photos appear on your iPhone, iPad, and Mac. There are no PhotoPots servers and no accounts to create.

What is the difference between Free and Pro?

Free gives you 25 sorts a week. Pro removes the limit, so you can sort as many photos as you like. That is the only difference.

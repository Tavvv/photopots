Why it matters

Some photos were never meant for your Camera Roll

Your library is for your life. These shots are for work, records, and proof — and they deserve their own home.

  • Work documentation

    Progress shots, before-and-afters, site conditions. They pile up fast, and none of them belong next to your kid's birthday photos.

  • Receipts & expenses

    Snap the receipt at the register, straight into the expenses pot. At tax time it's all in one place — not buried under 400 screenshots.

  • Whiteboards & notes

    Meeting whiteboards, lesson plans, serial numbers, wiring diagrams. Useful for a week, visual noise forever after — unless they live in a pot.

  • Privacy by default

    Patient photos, student work, client properties. Anything sensitive stays out of the personal library other apps can see, and out of shared albums and Memories.

  • Evidence photos

    Damage reports, deliveries, rental condition, accident scenes. Timestamped, GPS-stamped, and kept apart from everything personal.

The PhotoPots camera

Take photos without saving to the Camera Roll — here's the flow

It feels like the camera you already know. The difference is where the photo goes.

  1. Swipe to choose a pot

    Pots sit along the capture screen — Work, Receipts, Site 12, whatever you've named them, each with its own color. One swipe and the next shot has a destination.

  2. Capture

    Tap the shutter. The photo is written directly into the chosen pot. It never enters the Camera Roll, iCloud Photos, or Recently Deleted. Nothing to delete later, because there's nothing to delete.

  3. Crop and edit on the spot

    Straighten the whiteboard, crop out your thumb, adjust the light. The built-in editor keeps every byte of metadata intact — no stripped timestamps like you get from share-sheet workarounds.

  4. GPS metadata stays attached

    Location, date, and time are captured and preserved. For contractors and inspectors, that turns a photo into a record: this place, this day, this condition.

  5. One-tap share, anywhere

    Send the shot to Messages, Mail, WhatsApp, Slack, or Drive straight from the pot. The client gets the photo in seconds; your Camera Roll stays clean.

Already took shots with the regular Camera app? You can still sort them into pots — sorting moves the original out of your Camera Roll, it doesn't just hide it.

Questions

Camera-to-pot FAQ

Does the PhotoPots camera save photos to my Camera Roll?

No. When you shoot with the in-app camera, the photo is written straight into the pot you picked. It never enters your Camera Roll, iCloud Photos library, Memories, or Recently Deleted — there is nothing to clean up afterward.

What about photos I already took with the regular Camera app?

Sort them into a pot. PhotoPots moves the original out of your Camera Roll — it is removed from the library, not just hidden in an album. After sorting, the photo lives only in its pot.

Is GPS location still saved on pot photos?

Yes. GPS coordinates, date, and time are captured and preserved, and they survive the built-in crop and edit tools. That makes pot photos usable as documentation for job sites, inspections, and deliveries.

Can I share a photo right after taking it?

Yes. Every photo in a pot has one-tap sharing to Messages, Mail, WhatsApp, Slack, Drive, or any other app in the iOS share sheet — no detour through the Camera Roll.

Is the camera that skips the Camera Roll free?

Yes. The free plan includes 25 sorts or captures a week, which covers light use. Pro removes the limit for unlimited capture and sorting. See pricing — download PhotoPots free and upgrade only if you hit the cap.

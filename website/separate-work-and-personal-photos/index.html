The problem

Your Camera Roll was never meant to hold two lives

If you work with photos, mixing them with personal ones costs you more than tidiness.

  • Memories resurfaces everything

    iPhone Memories and slideshows pull from your whole library. That means a drywall tear-out or a wound-dressing photo can pop up in the "year in review" you play for your kids. Your Camera Roll doesn't know the difference between a moment and a deliverable — but you do.

  • Shared albums leak context

    Share a holiday album with family and your work shots are still sitting one swipe away in the same library. iCloud sharing, AirDrop suggestions, and "For You" recommendations all treat every photo as fair game.

  • Showing photos gets awkward

    Hand your phone to a client to show the finished kitchen and they're a thumb-swipe from your weekend. Or hand it to your kid to see a puppy picture and they're a swipe from a customer invoice. Privacy shouldn't depend on nobody swiping.

Why not albums?

Albums and the Hidden folder don't actually separate anything

The built-in fixes feel like separation, but they aren't. An album in the Photos app is just a view — sort a work photo into a "Jobs" album and the original still lives in your library. It still appears in Memories, in search, in shared suggestions, and in the "Recents" view everyone scrolls through.

The Hidden folder is closer, but it's built for hiding, not organizing. One long list, no categories, no color, no way to tell a plumbing invoice from a permit photo at a glance — and anything in it is still technically in the same library, one toggle away.

Real separation means the photo is gone from your personal library. That's exactly what PhotoPots does differently.

Get started

How to separate your photos in five minutes

No account, no import wizard. Here's the whole setup.

  1. Download PhotoPots and create your first pot

    Name it something obvious — "Job Sites", "Listings", "Patients" — and give it a color. Make a second pot for personal stuff if you want, or leave personal photos in the Camera Roll where they belong.

  2. Sort the backlog

    Scroll your Camera Roll inside PhotoPots and tap the work shots. Each sort moves the original out of your library into the pot. The free plan gives you 25 sorts a week — enough to chip away at the backlog, or go Pro for unlimited sorting and clear it in one sitting.

  3. Switch your habit to capture-to-pot

    From now on, take work photos with the PhotoPots camera. They land directly in the pot with GPS metadata preserved, and your Camera Roll stays personal by default.

  4. Triage your screenshots

    Run the batch screenshot tool to sweep receipts, whiteboard photos, and job details out of your library in bulk — sorted or deleted, your call.

  5. Share from the pot, not the library

    When a client, office, or insurer needs a photo, share it straight from the pot to Messages, Mail, or any app. Your personal photos never enter the conversation.

Questions

Separating work photos, answered

Do sorted photos disappear from my Camera Roll?

Yes — that's the point. When you sort a photo into a pot, PhotoPots moves the original out of your photo library. It isn't hidden in another album; it's genuinely gone from the Camera Roll, Memories, and shared albums, and it lives only inside the pot.

What's the difference between a pot and a regular photo album?

An album in the Photos app is a view — the original photo stays in your library, so it still shows up in Memories, searches, and shared albums. A pot is a separate container. Sorting into a pot removes the photo from your library entirely, and photos captured into a pot never enter it at all.

Can I capture work photos without them ever touching my Camera Roll?

Yes. PhotoPots has a built-in camera that saves each shot directly into the pot you choose. The photo never enters your Camera Roll, so there's nothing to clean up afterwards. GPS metadata is preserved, which matters for job-site and property documentation.

Will my pots sync to my iPad and Mac?

Yes. PhotoPots syncs your pots over iCloud, so the same work photos are waiting for you on iPhone, iPad, and Mac — sorted on one device, available on all of them.

Is PhotoPots free?

Yes. The free plan includes 25 sorts per week plus capture-to-pot, which is enough for most people to try the workflow. Pro removes the weekly limit for unlimited sorting. No account is required to start. See pricing or the full FAQ for details.
